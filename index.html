Forging forwards with web development

A little bit about me

I'm passionate about coding.

I'm Laura Faithfull and have a love for hills and adventure. My adventure into code started during Covid-19, when I was no longer able to work as an Osteopath during Scotland's first lockdown in spring 2020. Having worked in manual therapy for over a decade, computers weren't a regular part of life, but learning to code was exciting and a vital skill for keeping patients informed.

Coding is an addiction to order and clean lines. It's problem solving and thinking laterally, just like osteopathy. To create a website and see order from potential chaos is superb. To see a sleek website that is easy to use and looks good brings inspiration. It has tremendous power to support businesses.

My Portfolio

Community Osteopaths Website
HTML5 & CSS Website

Unable to trade during Covid-19's national lockdowns, my clinic - Community Osteopaths - needed a new, updated website to convey the necessary protocols for safe practice moving forwards. This website made booking in patients post-lockdown so much easier. It was invaluable. The website reflects the feel of our clinic: informative, reassuring and calming.

Vision Board Exercise
HTML & CSS

Learning to code through Skillcrush, they gave us a number of HTML and CSS exercises to hone our skills. This was one of them. Skillcrush was not my only resource. I also used CodeCamp and sites like Mozilla and w3 to broaden my knowledge.

Recipe Collection Exercise
HTML5 & CSS

Practicing these new computer languages is like learning anatomy, logical. Computational thinking is much akin to finding a patient's diagnosis: identify different body parts that have contributed to the patient's problem; filter through the order of treatment for their problem; recognise any patterns amongst the problem areas; use layman language/ pseudocode to relay to the patient their diagnosis and provide advice.

Rogue Pickings Exercise
HTML & CSS from Photoshop Design Comp

Working from a Design Comp is like studying a patient's movements... attention to detail is everything. Osteopathy processes the visual information presented by a patient's movements together with the verbal information gathered during a case history to find a solution to their problem. Front-end development allows me to make use of these vital skills.
